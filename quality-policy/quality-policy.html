Quality Management System

L & W Precision Machining LLC

This document describes the quality policies establed for L & W Precision Machining LLC.

Quality Policy

Our quality policy revolves around these these policy:

  • Understand and meet customers' current and future needs and expectations
  • Develop and deploy processes to ensure all personnel conform all established quality standards
  • Continuously maintain, review and improve the Quality Management System

Quality Objectives

We intend to meet these quality objectives:

  • Customer satisfaction ratings - 98%
  • Off-quality returns - 1%
  • On-time delivery of orders - 95%

All quality objectives are reviewed on a quarterly basis. Quality objective reviews will determine actual quality measurements per metric and compare these with the corresponding objective per time period. Any quality objectives that are not met will trigger appropriate corrective action.

Purchase Order Review

All proposed purchase orders will be reviewed to determine whether the company should continue with the contract. A purchase order review will identify requirements, estimate costs, and manage risks.

All purchase orders will be tracked and recorded according to any relevant terms and stipulations.

Measurement Traceability

All monitoring and measurement equipment adhere to these rules:

  • All measurement equipment are serialized and logged.
  • All measurement equipment are calibrated at specified time periods according to criteria traceable to accepted measurement standards.
  • The calibration of all measurement equipment is documented with calibration logs.
  • Measurement equipment found to be defective will be immediately marked as unusable and removed from use until repaired or disposed of.

Document Control

There are established methods for the development, revision, review, approval, distribution, maintenance and control of all documents. This process ensures that only authorized documents, templates and forms are used, that required documents are available to personnel where required and that obsolete copies or revisions are promptly removed, replaced, invalidated or destroyed.

All documents will be retained for 5 years, except for documents subject to other terms and obligations that require different retention periods.

Non-conforming Parts Control

All parts are checked for conformance to all relevant specifications and requirements throughout the manufacturing process.

  • Production: Non-conforming parts discovered during production are identified and segregated. Production personnel will determine whether the non-conforming parts may be reworked to conformance or scrapped and replaced with new parts.
  • First Article Quality Control: Non-conforming parts identified in any Quality Control phase will be sent back to the responsible production personnel, along with a quality report describing the non-conformance.
  • Final Inspection Quality Control: Non-conforming parts identified during final inspection routines will be recorded in production logs. Possible corrective action include reworking the non-conforming parts, producing replacement parts, and seeking customer advice on how to proceed.
  • After delivery: Communications will be maintained with customers that report non-conforming parts found after delivery. Corrective action may include return of the non-conforming parts for rework, replacement of the non-conforming parts with new parts, and modifications to the purchase order.

Corrective Action Process

Any and all nonconformances to quality objectives and process will be investigated in order to prevent their recurrance. Any and all nonconformances will be recorded and logged, and corrective action appropriate to the nature of the nonconformance will be taken.
